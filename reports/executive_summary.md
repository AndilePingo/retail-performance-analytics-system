# Executive Summary

## Retail Performance Analytics System

### Project Overview

This project analyzed the Global Superstore dataset to identify revenue drivers, profitability trends, customer behavior patterns, and operational improvement opportunities across a multinational retail business.

The analysis examined over 51,000 retail transactions and transformed raw sales data into actionable business insights designed to support strategic decision-making.

---

# Business Objective

The primary objective of this project was to answer the following business questions:

* Which markets generate the highest revenue and profit?
* Which product categories contribute most to business performance?
* Which customer segments are the most valuable?
* How do discounts impact profitability?
* Which products should be promoted, reviewed, or discontinued?

---

# Dataset Overview

| Metric       | Value          |
| ------------ | -------------- |
| Transactions | 51,290         |
| Orders       | 25,035         |
| Customers    | 1,590          |
| Revenue      | $12.64 Million |
| Profit       | $1.47 Million  |

The dataset includes information relating to:

* Sales
* Profit
* Product Categories
* Customer Segments
* Geographic Markets
* Shipping Information
* Discount Levels

---

# Data Quality Assessment

The dataset was assessed for completeness, consistency, and accuracy.

### Findings

* No duplicate records were identified.
* Date fields were converted to datetime format to support time-series analysis.
* Postal Code contained missing values for international records, which was expected due to differing regional data standards.
* The remaining dataset was suitable for analysis without major quality concerns.

# Key Findings

## 1. APAC is the Highest Performing Market

Revenue analysis revealed that the APAC market generated the highest revenue and profit among all markets.

### Top Markets by Revenue

| Market | Revenue |
| ------ | ------: |
| APAC   |  $3.59M |
| EU     |  $2.94M |
| US     |  $2.30M |

### Top Markets by Profit

| Market | Profit |
| ------ | -----: |
| APAC   |  $436K |
| EU     |  $373K |
| US     |  $286K |

This suggests that APAC represents a major growth opportunity and should remain a strategic focus area.

---

## 2. Technology is the Strongest Category

Technology products generated both the highest revenue and highest profit.

### Revenue by Category

| Category        | Revenue |
| --------------- | ------: |
| Technology      |  $4.74M |
| Furniture       |  $4.11M |
| Office Supplies |  $3.79M |

### Profit by Category

| Category        | Profit |
| --------------- | -----: |
| Technology      |  $664K |
| Office Supplies |  $518K |
| Furniture       |  $285K |

Technology products demonstrated superior profitability compared to other categories.

---

## 3. Consumer Customers Drive Business Growth

The Consumer segment generated the largest share of both revenue and profit.

### Revenue by Segment

| Segment     | Revenue |
| ----------- | ------: |
| Consumer    |  $6.51M |
| Corporate   |  $3.82M |
| Home Office |  $2.31M |

### Profit by Segment

| Segment     | Profit |
| ----------- | -----: |
| Consumer    |  $749K |
| Corporate   |  $441K |
| Home Office |  $277K |

The Consumer segment should remain a primary target for future marketing and customer acquisition efforts.

---

## 4. Discounts Negatively Impact Profitability

Correlation analysis identified a negative relationship between discount levels and profit.

Correlation Coefficient:

-0.316

This indicates that increasing discount levels are associated with lower profitability.

While discounts may help drive sales volume, excessive discounting may reduce overall business performance.

---

## 5. Product Profitability Varies Significantly

Several products generated substantial profits, while others generated significant losses.

### Most Profitable Product

Canon imageCLASS 2200 Advanced Copier

Profit Generated:

$25,199

### Largest Loss-Making Product

Cubify CubeX 3D Printer Double Head Print

Loss Generated:

-$8,880

These findings indicate opportunities to optimize product strategy and pricing decisions.

# Strategic Recommendations

## Recommendation 1

Increase investment in APAC markets through targeted marketing initiatives, inventory expansion, and customer acquisition campaigns.

---

## Recommendation 2

Prioritize Technology products due to their strong revenue generation and profitability performance.

---

## Recommendation 3

Review discounting policies to reduce margin erosion and improve overall profitability.

---

## Recommendation 4

Conduct further analysis on loss-making products to determine whether pricing, sourcing, inventory management, or marketing strategies should be adjusted.

---

## Recommendation 5

Continue focusing on Consumer customers, as they represent the company's most valuable customer segment.
